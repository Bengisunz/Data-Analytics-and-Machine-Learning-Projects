A. Introduction¶

About the Data¶

"The data set l chose is Airbnb Users data set that is offered for the recruiting competition. Airbnb New User Bookings data set is represented at Kaggle.com by Airbnb that is community marketplace for people to list, discover, and book unique accommodations around the world. The data set includes a list of users along with their demographics, web session records, and some summary statistics. All the users in this dataset are from the USA. "There are 12 possible outcomes of the destination country: 'US', 'FR', 'CA', 'GB', 'ES', 'IT', 'PT', 'NL','DE', 'AU', 'NDF' (no destination found), and 'other'. The training and test sets are split by dates. In the test set, you will predict all the new users with first activities after 7/1/2014 (note: this is updated on 12/5/15 when the competition restarted). In the sessions dataset, the data only dates back to 1/1/2014, while the users dataset dates back to 2010.

More about Airbnb¶

"Airbnb is an online marketplace and hospitality service, enabling people to lease or rent short-term lodging including vacation rentals, apartment rentals, homestays, hostel beds, or hotel rooms. The company does not own any lodging; it is merely a broker and receives percentage service fees (commissions) from both guests and hosts in conjunction with every booking. It has over 3,000,000 lodging listings in 65,000 cities and 191 countries, and the cost of lodging is set by the host.

How it works¶

Airbnb can be accessed via either the Airbnb websites or mobile applications for iOS, Apple Watch, and Android. Registration and account creation is free. On each booking, the company charges guests a 6%-12% guest services fee and charges hosts a 3%-5% host service fee. Hosts can also offer "experiences", such as excursions, to guests for an additional charge, of which Airbnb takes 20% as a commission.

Users can search for lodging using a variety of filters including lodging type, dates, location, and price. Before booking, users must provide a valid name, email address, telephone number, photo, payment information, and, if required by the host, a scan of a government-issued ID. Guests also are required to agree to any house rules of the host." [2]

Purpose and Objectives of the Project¶

This data is served for a Kaggle competition the aim was to predict for which country a new user will make his or her first booking. It can be suggested that Airbnb intends to know the journeys that the users who book have. It is possible to derive personas from the data. Each journey types help create a persona. In this way, the company invests its resources on the right users with the right ways. For example, if the users who book by using android mostly, the company may want to prioritize the topic of consumer experience on android. If insight can be gathered from the data, it leads action plans for the company.

As a hobby, I follow shared economy platforms and the developments in this area. Airbnb is one of them. Moreover, I like the idea of connecting people/cultures. 2 years ago I have watched this video that touched me as a person who lived in Berlin and put freedom first. I like the mission of Airbnb. Therefore, I chose to explore the Airbnb dataset. I wanted to comprehend the meaning of the dataset, find the differences and similarities in the preferences of subgroups. I hoped to find a meaningful insight. There might be an insight from the way people come to contact with Airbnb.

C. DISCUSSION & CONCLUSION¶

When we analyse the ages on the data set, the average age is 36, mode is 30, and the median is 33. The outliers on the age column are those older than 62. It is assumed that most of the users are neither very young nor very old. Number of females is higher than number of males. When we analyze the gender column almost with any feature, it is seen that number of females is always higher. It was expected that the numbers of male and female users were close. Whereupon, l did a desk research and found out that females plans and travels are more than males. You can read the news from here.

All the users in this dataset are from the USA. Most of the users book the place in the USA. It is assumed that most of the users booked from airbnb for the country trip. It is followd by France, Italy, United Kingdom, and Spain. It is assumed that most of the users sign up on web browsers. It can be assumed that they plan the accommodation in advance. This is not suprising because most of the online shopping occurs on web browsers. Eventually, we can say that most of the users sign up on web browsers with their information. Only IOS users sign up with facebook. It is assumed that mobile users do not want to type their register information so facebook is a fast way to sign in. Moreover, not all the android phone users have a high quality smart phones. Therefore, it is not easy to log in on with facebook these android phones. Most of the male users sign up with facebook while most of the female users sign up with their information. Most of the mobile users male.

I think Airbnb may be an alternative solution for accommodation. If a traveler can not find a hotel or a hostel easily while travelling then, s/he books from Airbnb at the current moment. Therefore, l checked the proportion of the same day booking, which means the users register and also book on the same day . As a result, I found out that 0,1% of the users make the booking on the same day as they register, which is not significant. This indicates that the users plan the accomodation in advance and they are not in a hurry.

Year to year analyses show us that there are more users who booked in 2013 and 2014 than other years. The data does no cover whole year of 2015.
